# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the test strategy, quality gates, and test automation for the project. They partner with developers and product teams to define acceptance criteria, ensure comprehensive coverage, and report quality risks before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy and quality standards
- Own test automation frameworks and ensure adequate test coverage
- Collaborate with developers on acceptance criteria and testing requirements
- Manage quality gates in the release pipeline
- Report quality metrics and risks to stakeholders
- Coordinate manual QA efforts when needed

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce testing cycle time through automation
- Maintain clear quality standards and coverage metrics

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with developers on feature testing
- Weekly quality reports and risk assessments
- Sprint planning participation for test effort estimation
- Release readiness sign-off communications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test requirements, acceptance criteria, and bug triage
- **Product Managers**: Align on quality expectations and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Provide testing estimates, report quality risks, and communicate release readiness
- **DevOps Engineers**: Integrate test automation into CI/CD pipelines
- **UX Designers**: Coordinate on usability testing and user acceptance testing

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user flows, wireframes, and ensure usability best practices are followed. They drive user research, feedback cycles, and advocate for the end-user experience throughout the product development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces, workflows, and interaction patterns
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Validate implementations against design specifications

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support issues through good design
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with product and engineering
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Usability testing reports and iterations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on user needs, requirements, and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications, answer implementation questions, and validate results
- **QA Lead**: Support usability testing and user acceptance criteria validation
- **Business Analysts**: Align on user workflows and business requirements
- **Stakeholders**: Present design concepts and gather feedback

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (when Agile/Scrum methodology is adopted) facilitate sprint ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They work to strengthen team delivery rhythm and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments to team progress
- Coach team members on agile best practices
- Protect the team from external distractions during sprints
- Track and communicate team velocity and sprint metrics
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and delivery predictability
- Create a sustainable and healthy team working environment
- Drive continuous process improvement through retrospectives

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and blocker tracking
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Regular check-ins with team members on impediments
- Metrics reporting to leadership

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Delivery Team**: Facilitate ceremonies, remove blockers, and coach on agile practices
- **Product Managers**: Ensure backlog readiness and clarity for sprint planning
- **Project Managers**: Align on timelines, dependencies, and cross-team coordination
- **All Roles**: Support collaboration and communication across the team

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers ensure CI/CD pipeline health, configure and maintain deployment environments, implement operational monitoring and logging, and support incident response. They bridge development and operations to enable reliable, automated delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure, deployment environments, and configuration
- Implement and monitor application observability (logging, metrics, alerts)
- Automate deployment processes and infrastructure provisioning
- Support incident response and post-incident analysis
- Ensure security and compliance in deployment processes

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and automated deployments
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce manual deployment effort and human error

### Typical Communication
- Deployment status updates and infrastructure changes
- Incident response coordination and post-mortems
- Pipeline and tooling improvements documentation
- Operational metrics and alerts

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide pipeline support, infrastructure guidance, and deployment tools
- **QA Lead**: Integrate automated testing into CI/CD pipelines
- **Project Managers**: Communicate deployment schedules and operational readiness
- **Security Team**: Implement security scanning and compliance controls
- **On-call/Support**: Coordinate incident response and provide operational insights

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, translate business needs into user stories and specifications, and bridge the gap between stakeholders and technical teams. They ensure clarity, traceability, and alignment throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into detailed user stories and specifications
- Create process flows, data models, and requirement documentation
- Facilitate requirement validation and acceptance criteria definition
- Track requirement traceability throughout delivery
- Support user acceptance testing and requirement sign-off

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and traceable requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Reduce rework through early requirement validation

### Typical Communication
- Requirement gathering sessions with stakeholders
- User story refinement with product and engineering teams
- Documentation of business processes and workflows
- Requirement validation and acceptance testing coordination

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Stakeholders**: Gather requirements, validate understanding, and manage expectations
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on product vision, priorities, and requirement details
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements, answer questions, and validate implementations
- **QA Lead**: Define acceptance criteria and support user acceptance testing
- **Project Managers**: Provide requirement documentation and support scope management
